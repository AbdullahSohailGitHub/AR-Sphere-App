The Core Problem

Why your sphere
interior disappears

Unity's default sphere mesh faces its normals outward. The moment your AR camera crosses the surface, it sees nothing — just a void or back-face flicker. This is a rendering pipeline problem, not a geometry problem. Here's what's actually happening:

🔵
Back-Face Culling
Unity's default shaders cull (hide) polygons whose normal points away from the camera. Inside the sphere, every normal points away — so everything vanishes.
🔴
The Red Rods Problem
Your red rods extend inside the sphere. Without correct interior rendering, they clip or disappear mid-entry. The fix must handle them too — not just the sphere surface.
📱
AR Foundation Depth
On Android via AR Foundation, the AR camera pose is tracked by ARCore. Entry/exit detection must work with world-space movement, not Unity's physics-based gravity.
⚡
No Rebuild Needed
Your brief says don't recreate the sphere module from scratch. Good — this is a targeted shader + trigger fix, not an architectural change. Your existing code stays intact.

My Approach

Three surgical changes.
Clean, commented, done.

I build on your existing sphere module. No rewrites. Every change is commented so you can follow exactly what I did and why.

1
Inward-Facing Normals + Double-Sided Shader
I'll apply a custom double-sided unlit shader (or flip normals on the mesh) so the sphere surface renders correctly from both outside and inside. I'll tell you exactly which approach I chose and why — likely double-sided to preserve your existing sphere generation code.
2
Sphere Trigger Collider + Entry/Exit Detection
A trigger collider wraps the sphere. A C# script detects when the AR camera's world position crosses inside — then toggles the prompt UI ("Walk in" → "Look around" → "Walk out"), ensuring the red rods remain visible throughout.
3
Screen Recording Proof of All Three States
I'll deliver a real Android screen recording showing the full flow: prompt appears → user walks in → interior surface visible → user walks out → exterior view restored. APK available on request.
